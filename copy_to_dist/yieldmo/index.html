My name is Adam. I grew up in a little town called Holland, in West Michigan. The greater Holland area is home to many tulips, a few windmills, and the Herman Miller headquarters. As a result of the latter, it is a land abounding with Eames Lounge Chairs and Steve Frykholm posters.
My mother is an English teacher. My father does construction remodeling and teaches jazz percussion. Basically, I grew up around a lot of books, music, and awesome power tools. I spent a lot of time reading, learning how to build & fix things, and playing in terrible rock bands.
In the 8th grade a family friend - an engineer at Microsoft at the time - handed down a used Dell machine and a modem, and I discovered the internet. My online life started with a Geocities site, trading Smashing Pumpkins bootleg concerts on cassette with people all around the world. A year later my best friend Sean and I started our first business. We made almost no money, but we built a few websites, and learned some great lessons that would come in handy for our next venture.
In 2003, we launched a new business - RetroDuck.com (the brainchild of my business partner). By mid-2004, we held the number one spot for a 'vintage t-shirts' query on Google, and were shipping t-shirts all over the world from a house near Michigan State University. Fast forward 12 years later; our fledgling vintage t-shirt site has grown into a brick and mortar screen printing company. I ran the company for about 6 years and left to focus on software, after a few years of building web apps for the company.
After about 18 months of freelance work, as well as a day job helping to plan a large database migration, I landed a 1 month contract at Google to rework a UI for a maps product. As the project grew, I was asked to continue work on some related products, and am still slinging Javascript in Mountain View today. I'll be relocating to New York in May 2015. I'm looking for a place where I can continue focusing on my front-end skills, and contribute to a creative atmosphere.
